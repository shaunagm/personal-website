Work

Since Spring 2010 I have worked as an independent consultant. All positions listed before that date are full-time; all positions listed after that date are part-time client work.

Founder, Galaxy Rise Consulting April 2015 - Present
I provide technical advice and development services to clients. Services include web and mobile development as well as data science and documentation.

In addition to growing the series from 2-3 events per year to 20-30 events per year, I handled a number of related tasks and projects.
Speaking ▼
I represented OpenHatch at a variety of conferences, meetups, and student events. Some of these are listed on my personal talks page.

I created, adapted and/or maintained a number of technical tools for OpenHatch. These include the the project's promotional site and event management site, curriculum tools such as Merge Stories and our version control activity, and a bot that welcomes people in the OpenHatch IRC channel.
Researcher, MetaMed November 2012 - June 2014
As a contract researcher for MetaMed, a personalized medical research company, I read, critiqued, summarized and did meta-analysis of the basic literature on medical conditions to aid professionals in diagnosis and treatment.
Data Analyst, BoxLab, MIT Media Lab January 2012 - June 2012
As a contract researcher on the BoxLab project, I processed and analyzed large quantities of audio, video and sensor data using primarily Python and C#. Unfortunately, funding for the project ran out before I could finish, and the materials available online are currently incomplete.
Developer, Harvard SEAS Lab January 2011 - March 2011

As an occasional instructor, I have led a variety of activities including roleplaying a cholera outbreak, making telegraphs, drawing a giant board game on the floor, playing baseball and computing baseball statistics, high speed photography, making and running a kids' puzzle hunt, and more.
Lab Manager, Moral Cognition Lab September 2008 - March 2010
The Moral Cognition Lab is a psychology research lab focused on studying moral judgement and decision making. As manager, I oversaw the design, implemenation, analysis and presentation of multiple experiments as well as the functioning of the Moral Cognition Lab as a whole.
Technical Development ▼

Junior Specialist, UC Davis Imaging Research Center September 2007 - June 2008
I assisted with research into emotion processing in people diagnosed with schizophrenia and substance abuse disorders. Responsibilities included recruiting and screening participants, performing structured clinical interviews with healthy control participants, collecting and analysing neuroimaging, physiological, and behavioral data, and managing financial and review board-related interactions and materials.
Instructor, Summer Institute for the Gifted Summer 2007, 2008, 2010

Education

Hampshire College September 2003 - June 2007
BA: Behavioral Neuroendocrinology
Thesis ▼
For my senior thesis (Div III) I explored the relationship between social and moral behavior, and physiological measures such as peripheral body temperature and the hormones cortisol, epinephrine, and testosterone. I conceived of, designed and ran an experiment looking at how the stress response impacts altruistic behavior. I also collaborated with another student on a study which explored the interactions between testosterone levels, punishing behavior, and electroencephalographic response. In addition to the two chapters which summarized this research, a third chapter contained a literature review of research into altruistic behavior and the stress response.
Additional Research ▼
In addition to my thesis work, I conducted several pilot studies exploring social emotions in the Hampshire College Event-Related Potential lab and the Amherst College Social Psychology lab. I also completed a summer internship with Frans de Waal at the Yerkes Primate Research Center, where I non-invasively studied Capuchin monkeys. In addition to learning how to care for and observe the monkeys and assisting with graduate student work, I also designed and implemented colorblindness tests which showed that several of the capuchins had visual impairments which potentially confounded laboratory research.
Teaching Assistantship ▼
In my final semester at Hampshire, I TA'd an introductory neuroscience class. In addition to leading a class session and several labs, I volunteered to mentor several students who wanted to do independent final projects and was responsible for evaluating them. The professor wrote of my work: 'I can honestly say that Shauna is one of the best TAs I have ever had.'
